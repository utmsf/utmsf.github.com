Mission

Our group is the Médecins Sans Frontières University of Toronto Chapter, a student organisation fully affiliated with Médecins Sans Frontières / Doctors Without Borders Canada. Our mandate is to help students on campus respond to their concerns of humanitarian crises by helping them become aware of MSF and their work around the world.

MSF is the world's leading independent medical humanitarian organisation that delivers emergency aid to people affected by armed conflicts, epidemics, disasters, and exclusion from health care in close to 60 countries worldwide. Although students may not volunteer yet with MSF field missions, however, knowing that MSF needs engineers, accountants, nurses, journalists, and doctors can shape a student's career and life choices.

The objective of the MSF U of T Chapter will be to pursue the following goals as they pertain to the MSF mission and charter:
(1) to draw attention to the value of international medical relief and humanitarian aid, specifically to the current field projects of MSF Canada;
(2) to educate students about international health issues, such as HIV/AIDS and tuberculosis, while simultaneously striving to engage students and the public in the international response to such issues; and
(3) to engage the student population in fundraising initiatives that support the work of MSF Canada.

These goals will be achieved with events that include guest speakers such as expatriates who have recently returned from the field, awareness campaigns that bring attention to MSF's current work and the issues they face like access to medicine, and fundraising activities which benefit MSF Canada.

History

The University of Toronto Chapter for the Friends of MSF was founded in 2008 to provide an additional venue of expression for humanitarian enthusiasts on campus. Originally comprised of mostly medical students, the Chapter has since extended its reach with students from multiple faculties. With a strong affiliation with MSF Canada, our chapter has fostered a more active and aware environment on campus through several keynote events including the Refugee Camp Out for a Cause, Trauma Workshop and Treats for Treatment. The Friends of MSF University of Toronto Chapter has established a tradition of excellence in hosting events to engage the local community in the discourse of humanitarian aid.

Frequently Asked Questions - About MSF

What is Médecins Sans Frontières/(MSF)?


MSF is an independent international medical organization that provides assistance to people caught up in crisis around the world. As well as providing medical care, MSF also "bears witness" to the plight of the people it assists when it thinks this can help those in crisis through talking to the authorities, to the UN, to key governments, by working with the press, and holding awareness events.

How do you decide where and when to go?


MSF carries out independent assessments in a crisis to decide whether it is needed and whether it can assist. MSF works independently of any political, military, or religious agendas. This means that it can respond based on need alone. As a medical humanitarian organization, the primary criterion for intervention is always medical need.

What is MSF's mission?


It is based on the principles of humanitarian action. That people caught in crisis deserve access to quality medical care and to be spared the excesses of violence. MSF has a charter that refers to providing assistance to all caught in crisis irrespective of who they are or where they live or what they believe in. This charter helps to define why we do what we do.

How many aid workers does MSF have?


At any one time there are about 24, 000 people in the field. Only 10% are international aid workers, the rest are hired locally.

Does MSF recruit only doctors?


MSF recruits doctors, including anesthesiologists and surgeons, nurses, laboratory scientists, mental health professionals, midwives, and water-and-sanitation specialists. MSF recruits also recruits non-medical staff to work in its projects, as financial controllers, project coordinators, logisticians and other technicians. All of these people bring professional abilities, practical experience, as well as a commitment to and concern for the people they help.

What are the recruiting criteria?


Two years professional experience and availability for a minimum of 9 months, except for surgeons and anesthesiologists that can be for as little as 4 weeks. Language skills, particularly French are considered a major asset as are relevant travel or work experience outside Canada. There are other qualifying factors, so refer to the website for details.

Do field staffs have to pay for the expenses to support themselves in the field?


MSF provides small monthly remuneration to its field workers, and covers all expenses related to transportation, insurance, and room and board.

How much of the money donated is actually spent on program activities?


More than 85% of the money donated goes to program activities - humanitarian assistance and raising awareness. The annual report is available online and in printed form.

Are there any groups that you don't accept donations from?


Yes. MSF has guidelines about who it cannot accept donations from because their activities may directly contradict MSF's work. MSF does not take donations from arms, pharmaceutical, alcohol, or tobacco companies, for example. Refer to the MSF website for more info.

Do you organize trips to volunteer with MSF?


Sadly, no we are not directly involved in any MSF missions or programs. MSF requires a minimum of two years work experience in a selective field. Our support for MSF takes place on campus.

How are you involved with MSF?


We support MSF Canada through fundraising and awareness events. Events with a fundraising component raise money for MSF Canada, either through sales or donations. Awareness events are designed to advocate the work of MSF, as well as educate the public on general global health, and current international health issues. We want to draw attention to the vale of MSF, and how it has improved the lives of millions! (Also: see our mission statement under "about us".
